When to send a W-9

Send your W-9 to a client when:

New client, first job
Send it before or right when they pay you for the first time. They need it to set you up in their system.
Client asks for it
Any time a client requests one — send it same day. Holding it up delays their payment to you.
You'll be paid $600 or more
If total payments from one client will hit $600+ in a calendar year, they're required to file a 1099 — and they need your W-9 to do that.
Your info changed
New address, name change, or you got an EIN — send an updated W-9 to any active clients so their records are current.
Don't send it to individuals paying you personally
W-9s are for businesses paying contractors. If a friend or family member pays you, no W-9 needed.
Never send it unsolicited to strangers
Your W-9 has your SSN on it. Only send it to clients you've already agreed to work with.
Pro tip — send it proactively
Don't wait for clients to ask. Include your W-9 in your onboarding email when a new project kicks off. It shows professionalism and prevents payment delays later.
